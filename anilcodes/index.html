My coding journey began in a zigzag way. In 1999, I went to New Delhi to work on building a website for a non-profit. I was supposed to get trained. But I was totally discouraged when a guy that I was supposed to be trained under told me how lousy my code looked.

It took me a decade and half to get my courage back and start learning again.

I am a self-taught developer. There is a caveat here. When I say self-taught, I meant that I did not go to a formal training programs: either at accredited college or at a bootcamp. Instead, I learned by using resources available online. In that sense, I learned because many people had created those resources for me to learn.

Therefore, strictly speaking, it was not a self-teaching, but rather free-teaching through self-initiative. Between 1999 when I was totally discouraged to learn because someone thought I was lousy, and 2015 when I finally got the courage to start learning, things had changed beyond recognition. Internet changes everything!

I got lots of academic training in the meantime. I am not sure if I will seek strictly academic jobs, but academic training was important for me to have the skills in understanding problems and finding solutions. As Einstein told us long ago, the main question is to understand the problem befofe we can tackle it properly.

Academia taught me about not to go for hasty solutions. It taught me to understand the problem deeply first before thinking about solutions. That is what comes handy as I transition into the world of programming.
